PISA 2012 - Student-Teacher Relationship¶

by Verena Dietrich¶

Dataset Overview¶

"PISA 2012 is the programmes 5th survey. It assessed the competencies of 15-year-olds in reading, mathematics and science (with a focus on mathematics) in 65 countries and economies. In 44 of those countries and economies about 85 000 students also took part in an optional assessment of creative problem solving; and in 18 countries and economies, students were assessed in financial literacy.

Around 510 000 students between the ages of 15 years 3 months and 16 years 2 months participated in PISA 2012 as a whole representing about 28 million 15-year-olds globally." [https://www.oecd.org/pisa/keyfindings/pisa-2012-results.htm, June 2021]

Investigation Overview¶

That the parents' involvement/engagement has highly positive impact on the children's success in school is known (https://www.oecd.org/pisa/49012097.pdf). I am wondering how the teacher's relationship influences the children's success and if there is a difference between femal eand male students.

In this context a relationship is defined as positive if the student gets along with the teacher, and the student has the feeling that the teacher is interested, listens, helps and treats the students fair. A relationship is defined as negative if this points are not given. The rating is devided into "strongly agree", "agree", disagree" and "strongly disagree" with the statements "Get along with teachers.", "Teachers are interested.", "Teachers listen to students.", "Teachers help students." and "Teachers treat students fair."

Further insights:¶

  • Female students have on average 3.5 points and male students have on average 2.7 points more out of 1000 points with a positive than with relationship to their teacher.

  • Additinally to the increased scores, the confidence intervall of the results is much smaller. Therefore, the success fo students with a positive relationship to the teachers is not only greater but also more stable.

  • It can be seen, that female students have slightly better results than male students.

Surprisingly, a very good student teacher relationship correlates with decreased scores¶

Summary¶

We could see, that a positive student teacher relationship correlates with better results.

We could also see, that a very good relationship correlates with decreased scores. We have to be careful and distinguish between causality and correlation. A possible explanation why a very good student teacher relationship seems to decrease the success of the students could be the school types. For the school types with students who have learning deficits could have a in general smaller classes and a stronger focus on a good student teacher relationship and therefore a better students teacher relationship, but lower scores per definition.
